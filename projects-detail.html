WASTE to WEALTH Project

NEED ASSESMENT
There are increased usage of plastic materials as packages or kitchen and office wear. These are non degradable materials. Degradation of the plastic waste is very slow process. Improper dispose of these waste such as into water bodies can result into killing of aquatic animals and terrestrial animals (i.e cows, goat). Spreading of these waste pollutes environment and attracts breeding of very dangerous insect in Africa known as mosquito. These dangerous insect spread a disease known as Malaria which kills many people especially infants and pregnant mother

JUSTIFICATION
Plastic waste will be recycled into paving blocks and floor tiles. Use of this product will replace cement and reduce the cost of paving block when compared to that of convention concrete paving blocks. The project is helpful in reducing plastic waste in a useful way.

PROJECT OUTPUT
Utilization of plastic waste in a production of paving block will be a productive way of disposing plastic waste. Plastic paving block is low cost, shows good heat resistance as compared to concrete paving block. This can be used in non-traffic and light traffic road.

CHILDREN VILLAGE PROJECT

CHALLENGES
Children aged 5-16 years lack good handling from their parents or care givers. They lack playing facilities for their brain development, insufficient balanced diet, moral training and thus results into poor academic performance and bad future generations. Since children learn through hearing and seeing, what they normally see and hear is all about violence, sexual harassment, drug abuse, bad manners (i.e insult words, poor life skills) and alcoholic behavior from their neighbors. Some parents used to employ house maids who also make some violence against the children.

JUSTIFICATION
The construction of buildings for CHILDREN VILLAGE PROJECT will enable us to enroll more children in need so that they acquire quality skills and knowledge a per our strategy into community development for poverty reduction. We will be happy to see more donors who will be interested to support us in construction of buildings.

OUR STRATEGIES
During the project (September to December) children will be happy to play, sing, learn English, science and social sciences, inverting science into reality (acquiring practical skills), gifts, teaching aids and tools. We invite any partners (such as teachers/facilitator) with interest for the children in this program. We will be happy if atleast 3 or 2 any foreign partners will be with us in this year program.

PURCHASE OF BAKERY EQUIPMENTS

BACKGROUND
Ludewa District is one of the four districts in Njombe region whose population is 133,218 people (Tanzania National Census of Humanities and Housing, 2012). Local people from this area are involved in traditional fishing along the coast of Lake Nyasa basin, subsistence farming and livestock rearing and there are small amount of artisan of gold and gem quality green tourmaline.

JUSTIFICATION
The purchase of bakery equipment will enhance commencement of production of baked goods and solve community problems.

OBJECTIVES
1. Poverty and hunger reduction through employment opportunities 2. Promoting community development through agribusiness 3. Improving consumer’s health and promoting food security. 4. To satisfy the community demands for bakery and confectionery products.

COMMUNITY WATER SUPPLY IN MANDA VILLAGE

BACKGROUND
Women and adolescent girls spend most of their time in searching for water. School girls (teens) are most vulnerable to this issue. This has resulted into poor academic performance to them. During searching for water, some adolescent girls and women were reported to have been raped.

People in this community depends water from Lake Nyasa where they directly fetch from that lake. This may resulted into disease outbreak. There are some seasonal rivers which have been reported to have dangerous animals such as crocodiles, hipopotumus and snakes.
